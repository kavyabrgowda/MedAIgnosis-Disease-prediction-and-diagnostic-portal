Respiratory Infections

Common Cold

A mild viral infection affecting the upper respiratory system.

Symptoms: Runny nose, sore throat, cough, mild fever, fatigue.

Precautions: Wash hands often, cover mouth while coughing, avoid close contact with infected people.

Influenza (Flu)

A contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses, leading to fever, body aches, and fatigue.

Symptoms: Fever, chills, body aches, sore throat, fatigue, coughing.

Precautions: Get vaccinated, wash hands regularly, avoid close contact with infected individuals.

COVID-19

A highly contagious respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, spreading mainly through respiratory droplets.

Symptoms: Fever, cough, shortness of breath, fatigue, loss of taste/smell, body aches.

Precautions: Wear a mask, maintain social distance, wash hands frequently, and avoid crowded places.

Pneumonia

An infection of the lungs caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi, leading to inflammation and fluid accumulation in the air sacs.

Symptoms: Cough, fever, difficulty breathing, chest pain, fatigue.

Precautions: Vaccinate against pneumonia-causing pathogens, avoid smoking, and practice good hygiene.

Tuberculosis (TB)

A bacterial infection primarily affecting the lungs, caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis, but can affect other parts of the body.

Symptoms: Persistent cough, night sweats, weight loss, fatigue, chest pain, blood in sputum.

Precautions: Complete the TB treatment regimen, wear a mask to prevent spreading, maintain good nutrition.

Bronchitis

Inflammation of the bronchial tubes, often caused by viral infections, leading to coughing and difficulty breathing.

Symptoms: Coughing with mucus, wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness.

Precautions: Avoid smoking, stay hydrated, use humidifiers, and wash hands regularly.

Gastrointestinal Infections

Food Poisoning

A condition caused by consuming contaminated food, often due to bacteria or viruses.

Symptoms: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal cramps.

Precautions: Wash hands regularly, cook food thoroughly, avoid raw or undercooked foods, store food properly.

Stomach Flu (Norovirus)

A viral infection causing inflammation of the stomach and intestines, commonly known as the stomach flu.

Symptoms: Vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps, fever, headache, fatigue.

Precautions: Wash hands frequently, disinfect surfaces, avoid close contact with infected individuals.

Hepatitis A

A liver infection caused by the Hepatitis A virus, commonly transmitted through contaminated food and water.

Symptoms: Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes), fatigue, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, nausea.

Precautions: Get vaccinated, wash hands after using the restroom, avoid consuming contaminated water or food.

Cholera

A bacterial infection causing severe diarrhea and dehydration, often caused by contaminated water.

Symptoms: Watery diarrhea, vomiting, dehydration, leg cramps.

Precautions: Drink clean, safe water, practice good hygiene, avoid raw seafood or undercooked food.

Gastroenteritis

An infection of the stomach and intestines, often caused by viruses, bacteria, or parasites.

Symptoms: Diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal pain, fever, nausea.

Precautions: Wash hands often, avoid unclean water or food, avoid close contact with infected individuals.

Celiac Disease

An autoimmune disorder where the ingestion of gluten damages the small intestine lining.

Symptoms: Abdominal pain, bloating, diarrhea, fatigue, weight loss.

Precautions: Avoid foods containing gluten, read labels carefully, follow a strict gluten-free diet.

Vector-Borne Diseases

Malaria

A life-threatening disease caused by parasites transmitted through the bites of infected Anopheles mosquitoes.

Symptoms: Fever, chills, sweating, headache, nausea, vomiting.

Precautions: Use insect repellent, sleep under mosquito nets, take anti-malarial medication when recommended.

Dengue Fever

A viral infection transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes, causing severe flu-like symptoms.

Symptoms: High fever, severe headache, pain behind the eyes, joint and muscle pain, rash.

Precautions: Eliminate mosquito breeding sites, use mosquito repellent, wear long sleeves and pants.

Zika Virus

A viral infection spread primarily by Aedes mosquitoes, causing mild symptoms but posing risks during pregnancy.

Symptoms: Fever, rash, joint pain, conjunctivitis (red eyes).

Precautions: Use mosquito repellent, wear protective clothing, avoid traveling to areas with Zika outbreaks if pregnant.

Chikungunya

A viral disease transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes, leading to severe joint pain and other flu-like symptoms.

Symptoms: High fever, joint pain, rash, muscle pain, headache.

Precautions: Avoid mosquito bites by using repellent and wearing protective clothing, eliminate mosquito breeding grounds.

Yellow Fever

A viral disease transmitted by Aedes and Haemagogus mosquitoes, leading to liver damage, jaundice, and bleeding.

Symptoms: Fever, chills, headache, back pain, nausea, vomiting, jaundice.

Precautions: Get vaccinated, use insect repellent, wear long sleeves and pants, avoid mosquito exposure.

Leishmaniasis

A parasitic disease transmitted by the bite of infected sandflies, causing skin lesions or affecting internal organs.

Symptoms: Skin sores, fever, weight loss, enlargement of liver or spleen.

Precautions: Use insect repellent, wear protective clothing, avoid outdoor exposure during dusk and dawn.

Skin Infections and Diseases

Fungal Infections

Infections caused by fungi that affect the skin, hair, and nails, leading to symptoms like itching, redness, and scaling.

Symptoms: Itchy, red, or scaly skin, blisters, and peeling.

Precautions: Keep skin clean and dry, avoid sharing personal items, use antifungal creams as prescribed.

Eczema

A chronic inflammatory skin condition causing itching, redness, and inflammation, often triggered by environmental factors.

Symptoms: Itchy, dry, cracked skin, red or inflamed patches.

Precautions: Moisturize regularly, avoid harsh soaps, manage stress, and avoid allergens.

Psoriasis

An autoimmune disease that causes the rapid growth of skin cells, leading to thick, scaly patches of skin.

Symptoms: Red, inflamed skin with silvery scales, dry or cracked skin that may bleed.

Precautions: Use prescribed topical treatments, avoid triggers like stress, and moisturize skin regularly.

Scabies

A contagious skin condition caused by tiny mites that burrow into the skin, causing intense itching and rashes.

Symptoms: Intense itching, especially at night, red rash, sores from scratching.

Precautions: Avoid close physical contact, use prescribed topical treatments, wash clothing and bedding regularly.

Acne

A skin condition characterized by the occurrence of pimples, blackheads, and cysts, often caused by hormonal changes and clogged pores.

Symptoms: Pimples, blackheads, cysts, or whiteheads on the face, chest, or back.

Precautions: Keep skin clean, avoid squeezing pimples, use non-comedogenic skincare products, and consider topical treatments.

Impetigo

A highly contagious bacterial skin infection that causes sores and blisters, often around the mouth or nose.

Symptoms: Red sores that quickly burst and form a honey-colored crust, itching.

Precautions: Keep wounds clean and covered, avoid touching infected areas, and wash hands frequently.

Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs)

HIV/AIDS

A chronic condition caused by the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV), leading to immune system damage and increased susceptibility to infections.

Symptoms: Fever, swollen lymph nodes, fatigue, weight loss, frequent infections.

Precautions: Use protection during sex, avoid sharing needles, get tested regularly, take pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) if at risk.

Gonorrhea

A bacterial infection primarily affecting the genital tract but can also infect the throat, eyes, or rectum.

Symptoms: Painful urination, pus-like discharge from genitals, testicular or pelvic pain.

Precautions: Use protection during sex, avoid unprotected sexual contact, get regular STI screenings.

Syphilis

A bacterial infection transmitted through sexual contact, progressing in stages with potentially severe health consequences if untreated.

Symptoms: Painless sore on genitals or mouth (early stage), rash, fever, swollen lymph nodes (later stages).

Precautions: Use protection during sex, avoid unprotected sexual contact, get regular STI screenings.

Chronic Diseases

Diabetes

A chronic disease characterized by high levels of sugar (glucose) in the blood due to the body's inability to produce or effectively use insulin.

Symptoms: Frequent urination, excessive thirst, extreme fatigue, and slow healing of wounds.

Precautions: Maintain a balanced diet, exercise regularly, monitor blood sugar levels, and take prescribed medications.

Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

A condition where the blood pressure in the arteries is persistently elevated, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Symptoms: Often asymptomatic, but severe cases may cause headaches, shortness of breath, or nosebleeds.

Precautions: Reduce salt intake, maintain a healthy weight, exercise regularly, and manage stress.

Heart Disease

A range of conditions affecting the heart, including coronary artery disease, arrhythmias, and heart failure.

Symptoms: Chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, or irregular heartbeat.

Precautions: Eat a heart-healthy diet, avoid smoking, manage blood pressure, and exercise regularly.

Asthma

A chronic respiratory condition that causes inflammation and narrowing of the airways, leading to breathing difficulties.

Symptoms: Wheezing, coughing, shortness of breath, and chest tightness.

Precautions: Avoid triggers like allergens, use prescribed inhalers, and follow a treatment plan from a healthcare provider.

Chronic Kidney Disease

A condition where the kidneys are damaged and cannot filter blood properly, leading to a build-up of waste and fluid in the body.

Symptoms: Fatigue, swollen ankles, poor appetite, and difficulty concentrating.

Precautions: Control blood pressure and diabetes, avoid excessive salt intake, and follow a kidney-friendly diet.

Neurological and Mental Health Disorders

Depression

A mood disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and a lack of interest in daily activities.

Symptoms: Feelings of worthlessness, fatigue, changes in appetite, and difficulty concentrating.

Precautions: Regular exercise, therapy, medication (antidepressants), and maintaining a support system.

Anxiety

A mental health condition characterized by excessive worry, fear, and apprehension about everyday situations.

Symptoms: Restlessness, rapid heartbeat, difficulty concentrating, and trouble sleeping.

Precautions: Practice relaxation techniques, engage in physical activity, therapy, and take prescribed medications.

Alzheimer’s Disease

A progressive neurodegenerative disorder that causes memory loss, confusion, and changes in behavior, typically affecting older adults.

Symptoms: Memory loss, difficulty recognizing familiar faces, confusion, and changes in mood.

Precautions: Medication for cognitive support, a structured routine, and support from caregivers.

Parkinson’s Disease

A progressive nervous system disorder that affects movement, causing tremors, stiffness, and balance problems.

Symptoms: Tremors, muscle rigidity, slow movements, and difficulty with balance and coordination.

Precautions: Physical therapy, medications like levodopa, and lifestyle changes to improve mobility and quality of life.

Bipolar Disorder

A mental health disorder characterized by extreme mood swings, including episodes of mania and depression.

Symptoms: Elevated mood, excessive energy, irritability, and drastic shifts between manic and depressive states.

Precautions: Regular therapy, mood-stabilizing medications, and maintaining a stable routine.

Schizophrenia

A severe mental disorder that affects thinking, emotions, and behavior, often causing delusions and hallucinations.

Symptoms: Hallucinations, delusions, disorganized thinking, and social withdrawal.

Precautions: Antipsychotic medications, therapy, and strong family and social support.

Musculoskeletal Disorders

Arthritis

A condition that causes inflammation in the joints, leading to pain, stiffness, and swelling, often affecting mobility.

Symptoms: Joint pain, swelling, stiffness, and decreased range of motion.

Precautions: Regular exercise, joint protection, pain management, and medications like NSAIDs.

Osteoporosis

A condition where bones become weak and brittle, making them more prone to fractures.

Symptoms: Fragile bones, frequent fractures, back pain, and loss of height.

Precautions: Calcium and vitamin D supplementation, weight-bearing exercises, and medications to strengthen bones.

Rheumatoid Arthritis

An autoimmune disorder that causes inflammation and deformity in the joints, particularly affecting the hands and feet.

Symptoms: Joint pain, swelling, stiffness, and fatigue, often worse in the mornings.

Precautions: Early treatment with disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs), physical therapy, and joint protection.

Osteoarthritis

A degenerative joint disease caused by the breakdown of cartilage, leading to pain, swelling, and decreased movement in the affected joints.

Symptoms: Joint pain, stiffness, swelling, and decreased flexibility, particularly in the knees, hips, and hands.

Precautions: Weight management, regular exercise, physical therapy, and pain management strategies.

Gout

A form of arthritis caused by a buildup of uric acid crystals in the joints, leading to sudden and severe pain, often in the big toe.

Symptoms: Intense pain, redness, and swelling in the affected joint, typically in the lower extremities.

Precautions: Limit alcohol and purine-rich foods, maintain a healthy weight, and take medications to lower uric acid levels.

Scoliosis

A condition where the spine curves sideways, often during the growth spurt before puberty, leading to back pain and postural changes.

Symptoms: Uneven shoulders, a visible curve in the spine, and back pain.

Precautions: Regular spinal screenings, physical therapy, and in severe cases, surgery or bracing.
